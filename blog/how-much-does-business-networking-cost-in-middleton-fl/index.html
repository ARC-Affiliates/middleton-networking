Why Do You Need an LLC Before Networking in Florida?

A Florida LLC is a Limited Liability Company registered with the Florida Division of Corporations that legally separates personal and business assets.

You don't strictly need an LLC to network in Florida, but most paid networking groups and directories require proof of a registered business entity, which costs $125 to file in 2026.

According to the Florida Division of Corporations (the state agency that registers business entities at sunbiz.org), LLC filing fees remain $125 as of 2026, with annual reports due by May 1 at $138.75. Florida Statute 605 governs LLC formation (see flsenate.gov). Middleton Networking advises new members to register their LLC before applying to referral chapters — most require a valid EIN, certificate of liability insurance, and active sunbiz.org registration. Sole proprietors can still list on free directories, but paid tiers and referral groups nearly always require an LLC or corporation.
